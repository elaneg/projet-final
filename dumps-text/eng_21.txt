   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
   Low flat-bottomed container for growing microbes

   In microbiology, a culture plate is a low flat-bottomed laboratory
   container for growing a layer of organisms such as bacteria, molds, and
   cells on a thin layer of nutrient medium. The most common types are the
   petri dish^[1]^[2] and multiwell plates.^[3]
   [330px-Multiwell_cell_culture_plates-set.jpg] Multiwell culture
   plates[250px-Penicillia_on_Petri_dish.jpg] Penicillium mold colonies in
   a Petri dish
